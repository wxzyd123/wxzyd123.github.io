Exploring the Vulnerability of Speech Tokenizer for Privacy Protection
against Illegal LLM-based Speech Synthesis

Authors

Submitted to ICASSP 2026

Abstract: Speech synthesis based on large language models (LLMs) has facilitated our lives, such as voice assistants. However, malicious deepfake speech also introduces security threats, e.g., telecom fraud. Existing defense mainly focuses on protecting at the timbre level, making the synthesized audio dissimilar while still preserving its usability. In this paper, we focus on the advanced LLM-based speech synthesis techniques and propose a speech protection framework named ST-Guard. We explore the vulnerability of the speech tokenizer and propose a proactive defense that can effectively prevent models from generating semantically meaningful speech. For the timbre protection, we employ the speaker encoder ensemble technique to enhance effectiveness and cross-model transferability. Furthermore, we optimize perturbative perception based on the psychoacoustic model for better imperceptibility. We conduct evaluations on three LLM-based speech synthesis models and one audio foundation model. Experimental results demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance on multi-speaker and multilingual datasets. Demo is available at https://wxzyd123.github.io/st-guard.
